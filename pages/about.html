About WWE & TKO Group Holdings

The global leaders in sports entertainment

World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE)

The premier professional wrestling and sports entertainment company in the world

Global Entertainment Powerhouse

World Wrestling Entertainment, Inc. (WWE) is an integrated media and entertainment company that has been delivering world-class content to a global audience for over 40 years. The company is primarily known for professional wrestling, but has expanded into various fields including film, American football, and various other business ventures.

WWE's programming reaches more than 900 million homes worldwide in 28 languages through world-class distribution partners including NBCUniversal, FOX Sports, BT Sport, Sony India and Rogers. The award-winning WWE Network includes all live pay-per-views, scheduled programming and a massive video-on-demand library and is currently available in more than 180 countries.

As of 2025, WWE continues to evolve its content strategy, focusing on premium live events, streaming partnerships, and expanding its global footprint while maintaining its position as the leader in sports entertainment.

TKO Group Holdings

The merger of WWE and UFC creating a global sports entertainment powerhouse

A New Era in Sports Entertainment

TKO Group Holdings, Inc. was formed in September 2023 through the merger of Endeavor's Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) and World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE). This historic merger created one of the largest publicly traded sports and entertainment companies in the world, with a combined enterprise value of over $21 billion.

As a unified company, TKO leverages the global reach, passionate fan bases, and complementary content offerings of both WWE and UFC to maximize value for shareholders, partners, and the millions of fans around the world. The company benefits from significant operating synergies, enhanced content creation capabilities, and expanded distribution channels.

Under the TKO umbrella, both WWE and UFC maintain their distinct brand identities while collaborating on strategic initiatives that strengthen their positions in the evolving media landscape. The company continues to innovate in content delivery, fan engagement, and global expansion, setting new standards for sports entertainment worldwide.

WWE History Timeline

The evolution of sports entertainment

1953

Capitol Wrestling Corporation (CWC) is founded by Jess McMahon and Toots Mondt, laying the foundation for what would eventually become WWE.

1963

The World Wide Wrestling Federation (WWWF) is established after breaking away from the National Wrestling Alliance (NWA).

1979

The company is renamed to World Wrestling Federation (WWF) as it begins expanding nationally.

1982

Vince McMahon Jr. purchases the company from his father and begins transforming it into a national promotion.

1985

The first WrestleMania is held at Madison Square Garden, marking the beginning of WWE's flagship annual event.

1993

Monday Night Raw debuts on USA Network, becoming one of the longest-running weekly episodic television shows in history.

1997-2002

The "Attitude Era" transforms wrestling with edgier content and iconic superstars like "Stone Cold" Steve Austin, The Rock, and D-Generation X.

2002

The company is renamed to World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) following a lawsuit from the World Wildlife Fund.

2008

WWE programming shifts to PG-rated content, focusing on family-friendly entertainment.

2014

WWE Network launches as a subscription-based streaming service, revolutionizing how fans consume wrestling content.

2019

SmackDown moves to FOX in a landmark television deal worth over $1 billion.

2023

WWE merges with UFC under Endeavor to form TKO Group Holdings, creating a global sports entertainment powerhouse.

2024

WWE content moves to Netflix in a major streaming deal, further expanding its global reach.

WWE Entertainment Offerings

A diverse portfolio of content and experiences

Television Programming

WWE produces several weekly television shows including Monday Night Raw, Friday Night SmackDown, and NXT, reaching hundreds of millions of viewers worldwide through broadcast partners and streaming platforms.

Premium Live Events

WWE hosts monthly premium live events (formerly pay-per-views) including WrestleMania, Royal Rumble, SummerSlam, and Survivor Series, which attract global audiences and generate significant revenue.

WWE Network & Streaming

WWE's content is available on various streaming platforms including Netflix, Peacock (in the US), and WWE Network (internationally), offering subscribers access to live events, original programming, and an extensive content library.

Live Events

WWE produces over 300 live events annually across the globe, allowing fans to experience the excitement of WWE in person through arena shows, stadium events, and international tours.

WWE Studios

WWE Studios produces feature films, documentaries, and television shows featuring WWE Superstars and original content for various distribution platforms.

Consumer Products

WWE's merchandise includes action figures, apparel, video games, home entertainment, and collectibles, available through retailers worldwide and WWE's online shop.

Digital & Social Media

WWE maintains a massive digital presence with over 1 billion social media followers and billions of video views annually across platforms including YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, X (formerly Twitter), and Facebook.

WWE 2K Games

The popular WWE 2K video game franchise allows fans to control their favorite Superstars in realistic wrestling simulations, with annual releases featuring updated rosters and gameplay innovations.
