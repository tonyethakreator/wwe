Biography

Jey Uso has transformed from one half of a legendary tag team into a bona fide singles star in WWE. Born Joshua Samuel Fatu, Jey has emerged from the shadow of his family legacy to forge his own path while still honoring his Samoan wrestling heritage.

As of September 2025, Jey is a fan-favorite on the Raw brand, having separated from The Bloodline faction in 2023 when he famously declared "I'm out!" and walked away from his cousin Roman Reigns' control. This moment of defiance catapulted Jey to new heights of popularity, with fans embracing his "YEET!" catchphrase and independent spirit.

Jey's in-ring style combines high-flying maneuvers with hard-hitting strikes, making him a versatile performer. While he achieved tremendous success as a tag team competitor alongside his twin brother Jimmy, his singles run has shown his ability to connect with audiences on a deeper emotional level.

His journey from being in Roman Reigns' shadow to standing on his own has been one of the most compelling character arcs in recent WWE history. Jey's authentic personality and connection with fans have made him one of the most beloved superstars on the roster.

Championship History

  • WWE Intercontinental Championship (1 time, current)
  • WWE SmackDown Tag Team Championship (5 times) - with Jimmy Uso
  • WWE Raw Tag Team Championship (3 times) - with Jimmy Uso
  • WWE Tag Team Championship (2 times) - with Jimmy Uso
  • FCW Florida Tag Team Championship (1 time) - with Jimmy Uso

After establishing himself as one of the greatest tag team wrestlers of all time alongside his brother Jimmy, Jey Uso broke through as a singles competitor in 2024, capturing the Intercontinental Championship at SummerSlam 2025.

Career Highlights

  • 2010: Debuted on WWE's main roster with his brother Jimmy
  • 2014: Won their first WWE Tag Team Championship
  • 2017: Underwent a character transformation, adopting a more serious "Uso Penitentiary" persona
  • 2020: Joined The Bloodline alongside Jimmy Uso and Roman Reigns
  • 2020: Had a critically acclaimed Hell in a Cell match against Roman Reigns
  • 2021: Captured the SmackDown Tag Team Championships inside Hell in a Cell
  • 2022: Won the Raw Tag Team Championships, becoming Undisputed WWE Tag Team Champions
  • 2023: Left The Bloodline with his iconic "I'm out!" moment
  • 2024: Won the Money in the Bank ladder match
  • 2025: Defeated Gunther for the Intercontinental Championship at SummerSlam
