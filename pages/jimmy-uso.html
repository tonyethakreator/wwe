Biography

Jimmy Uso is one half of the legendary Uso tag team and a member of the prestigious Anoa'i wrestling family. Born Jonathan Solofa Fatu, Jimmy has established himself as one of the most accomplished tag team wrestlers in WWE history alongside his twin brother Jey.

As of September 2025, Jimmy is aligned with his cousin Roman Reigns as part of The Bloodline faction. After a period of tension and separation from the group in 2023-2024, Jimmy returned to The Bloodline's fold, once again serving as Roman's right-hand man in their quest to dominate WWE.

Jimmy's in-ring style combines high-flying maneuvers with hard-hitting strikes, making him a versatile performer. His partnership with his brother Jey revolutionized tag team wrestling in WWE, bringing a new level of excitement and innovation to the division.

Outside the ring, Jimmy is married to fellow WWE superstar Naomi (Trinity Fatu), adding another layer to his wrestling legacy. His family connections run deep in the business, with his father Rikishi being a WWE Hall of Famer and his cousins Roman Reigns, Solo Sikoa, and Jacob Fatu all being prominent WWE superstars.

Championship History

  • WWE SmackDown Tag Team Championship (5 times) - with Jey Uso
  • WWE Raw Tag Team Championship (3 times) - with Jey Uso
  • WWE Tag Team Championship (2 times) - with Jey Uso
  • FCW Florida Tag Team Championship (1 time) - with Jey Uso

Jimmy Uso and his brother Jey hold the record for the most tag team championship reigns in WWE history, cementing their legacy as one of the greatest tag teams of all time.

Accomplishments

  • Slammy Award for Tag Team of the Year (2014, 2015) - with Jey Uso
  • WWE Year-End Award for Tag Team of the Year (2017) - with Jey Uso
  • Ranked No. 7 of the top 50 tag teams in WWE history (with Jey Uso)
  • First tag team to win both the Raw and SmackDown Tag Team Championships (with Jey Uso)
  • Part of the historic Bloodline storyline (2020-present)

Career Highlights

  • 2010: Debuted on WWE's main roster with his brother Jey
  • 2014: Won their first WWE Tag Team Championship
  • 2017: Underwent a character transformation, adopting a more serious "Uso Penitentiary" persona
  • 2020: Joined The Bloodline alongside Jey Uso and Roman Reigns
  • 2021: Captured the SmackDown Tag Team Championships inside Hell in a Cell
  • 2022: Won the Raw Tag Team Championships, becoming Undisputed WWE Tag Team Champions
  • 2023: Turned on Roman Reigns at Night of Champions, temporarily leaving The Bloodline
  • 2024: Reunited with The Bloodline under Roman Reigns' leadership
  • 2025: Continues to be a key figure in The Bloodline's dominance of WWE
